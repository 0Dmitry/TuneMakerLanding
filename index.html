Music Composition Lab

Composing music can be much more simple and efficient if your workflow and toolset help you to focus on the essential elements of music. Tune Maker teaches you the fundamental ingredients of music and gives you all necessary tools to perform your tune-making experiments.

Learn the basics

A project (a tune) in Tune Maker consists of one or more sections. Each section has 4 fundamental components: Drums, Chords, Arpeggio (chords rhythm pattern) and Melody.

Feel the beat

There is not any strict sequence of steps in music composition, but if you are just starting, you can begin by adding some drums to your tune. It will add basic structure and a mood to your future composition.

Hear the melody

After you have added some drums, you can add a melody. Add some notes to the melody track. Listen how the melody sounds along the drums. Continue adding notes to the melody until you happy with it.

Achieve the harmony

When you have your melody you can try adding chords. In music theory, the process of adding chords to a melody is called harmonization. Tune Maker will help you to find chords that match your melody. You specify the rhythm pattern of your chords by creating an Arpeggio track. Think of the Arpeggio track as a right-hand(your picking hand) pattern when you play on a guitar. You should always create an Arpeggio track along with the Chords track. If you only add chords, it is like pressing down chords on a guitar with you left hand but doing nothing with your right hand.

Build a complete song

Tune Maker allows you to create any number of sections in your project. You can duplicate existing section and add some alterations to it or you can just repeat a section. E.g if you have some kind of chorus section in your song, you could repeat it in multiple places inside your project. And if you decide to modify this section later, the changes will affect all the repetitions.

Organize, share & export

Tune Maker is a Document Based App and supports Open in Place feature. This means you can organize your projects in the way that works best for you. You can store documents on your device, in a folder on iCloud Drive, or in any other supported cloud storage like DropBox or GoogleDrive. You can also export your tune as a MIDI file if you want to continue working on it in some DAW like Ableton or GarageBand.
