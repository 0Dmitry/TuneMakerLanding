Music Composition Lab

Composing music can be much simpler if your workflow and toolset help you focus on the essentialÂ things. Tune Maker teaches you the fundamental ingredients of music and gives you all necessary tools to perform your tune-making experiments.

Feel the beat

There is not any strict sequence of steps in music composition, but if you are just starting, you can begin by adding some drums to your tune. It will create a basic structure of the future composition.

Hear the melody

Try to hear a melody in your head or sing it first. Then start drawing it on the melody track canvas. Hit play and listen while you are working on it. To make it more dynamic, use the velocity tool to adjust volume of individual notes.

Harmonize

In music, the process of adding chords is called harmonization, Tune Maker allows you to construct almost any possible chord and it will help you to find chords that match your melody.

Enliven chords with the Arpeggio

In Tune Maker, Arpeggio track is a rhythmic pattern for your chord progression. You can think of it as a right hand pattern when you play on a guitar. It can be like strumming, bass & chord or a single-note pattern. Tune Maker automatically maps this pattern to your chords and produces corresponding music notes.

Build a complete song

You can duplicate existing section and add some alterations to it or you can just repeat a section. E.g if you have some kind of chorus section in your song, you could repeat it in multiple places inside your song. And if you decide to modify this section later, the changes will affect all the repetitions.

Organize, share & export

Tune Maker is a Document Based App, and supports Open in Place feature. This allows you to organize your projects in the way that works best for you. You can store your projects on a local folder, on iCloud Drive, or on any other supported cloud storage like DropBox or GoogleDrive. You can share easily using AirDrop, Mail or Message. You can also export your project as a MIDI file if you want to continue working on it in a DAW like Ableton or GarageBand.
